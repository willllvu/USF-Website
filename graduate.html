Business Administration


Students attending the graduate program in the Muma College of Business have the chance to pursue either a PhD in Business Administration or become a Doctor of Business Administration.

A DBA - or Doctor of Business Adminstration - degree was original for students to learn how to teach and do research at business years. It has evolved over the past few years and is now designed to help working professionals pursue this degree. The program gives students the skills necessary to perform research and apply their findings to senarios in the real-world. They get to work with faculty in seminars and projects to develope these skills and to be able to communicate them to wide audiences. What graduates learn in the DBA program "uniquely qualifies them to serve in clinical faculty positions."

The Business Administration doctoral program is intended to prepare students to go into teaching, research, or as staff in industry and the government. Students learn through projects, seminars with faculty, and other activities that help build teaching and research skills. The curriculum specializes in the components of business administration along with enough specialization in the topic to allow students to make "meaningful contributions to the discipline." Students are also able to build upon their individual strenghts and interests they might have in various fields.
